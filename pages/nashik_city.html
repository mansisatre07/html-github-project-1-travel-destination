Harihar Fort:

         

Harihar Fort (Harshagad) is a historic fort in India's Nashik district, Maharashtra, known for its steep, vertical rock-cut steps and triangular rock formation, making it a popular trekking destination. Built by the Yadava dynasty to guard Gonda Ghat trade routes, the fort features religious idols and water cisterns. Accessible from base villages like Harshewadi and Nirgupada, it offers panoramic views of other Sahyadri forts from its summit at 3,676 feet.
