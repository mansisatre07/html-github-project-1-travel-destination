Harihar Fort!!

                  

About:

Harihara is famous for Harihareshwara Temple and Thungabadhra River.Harihar Fort (Harshagad) is a historic fort in India's Nashik district, Maharashtra, known for its steep, vertical rock-cut steps and triangular rock formation, making it a popular trekking destination. Built by the Yadava dynasty to guard Gonda Ghat trade routes, the fort features religious idols and water cisterns.
Accessible from base villages like Harshewadi and Nirgupada, it offers panoramic views of other Sahyadri forts from its summit at 3,676 feet.

History:

Harihar Fort was built between the 9th and 14th centuries by the Yadava dynasty. It was later captured by the Marathas in the 17th century, then by the Mughals, and finally by the British in 1818. The fort is renowned for its unique architecture, including nearly vertical rock-cut stairs designed to confuse enemies, and its strategic location on a triangular rock formation in the Nashik district of Maharashtra

9th–14th Century: Construction by the Yadava dynasty to protect their territory and trading routes.
17th Century: The Marathas took control of the fort.
1689:The fort was captured by the Mughal commander Matabbar Khan.
1818: The British, under Captain Briggs, captured the fort. Briggs was so impressed by the unique rock-cut stairs that he ordered them to be preserved instead of destroyed.

Ramkund!!

                 

About:

Ramkund is located along the bank of Godavari River. This place is situated at a distance of 2 km from Central Bus stand.
This is the holiest spot in Nashik as it is believed to be the place where Lord Rama used to bathe. It contains the bone dissolving Asthivilaya Tirth. It was built by Chitrarao Khatav, a landholder of Khatav in Satara in 1696havrao and was repaired by Gopikabai, the mother of Madhavrao the fourth Peshva. Peoples bring ashes of their deceased relatives and immerse it in Asthivilay kund. Ashes of big personalities like Pandit Nehru, Indira Gandhi, Y B Chavan and others had been immersed at Ramkund

History:

Ramkund is a sacred bathing tank in Nashik, built by Chitrarao Khatarkar in 1696 AD, where Lord Rama is believed to have bathed during his exile and performed his father Dasharatha's final rites. Located on the Godavari River, it is also known as the Asthi Vilaya Tirth or "bone dissolving tank," where people immerse the ashes of their deceased relatives. The area is also a central location for the Kumbh Mela, a major Hindu festival that occurs every 12 years.

Swaminarayan Temple!!

                

About:

The Swaminarayan Mandir in Nashik is located in Kevadivan, Panchavati, behind the Dental College, and is a grand stone-carved temple built in the Nagara style. It is open from 7:30 AM to 8:15 PM, with a break from 12:00 PM to 4:00 PM. The temple is known for its spiritual significance and intricate architecture, housing statues of deities like Radha, Krishna, Sita, Ram, and others.
Mangla Aarti: 6:00 AM
Shangar Aarti: 7:30 AM
Rajbhog Aarti: 11:15 AM
Sandhya Aarti: 7:00 PM
Shayan Aarti: 8:15 PM

History:

The history of Swami Narayan temples begins with Bhagwan Swaminarayan, who founded the sect and oversaw the construction of the first temple in Ahmedabad in 1822. He built a total of six temples during his lifetime, with the Ahmedabad location being the first to be built on land granted by the British East India Company. These mandirs were built to support the correct theological worship of God and to embody the spiritual purity and peace of the divine.

Peruchi Wadi Misal!!

               

              

Peruchi Wadis opening hours are from 08:30 to 16:00. We arrived at the restaurant at 4 pm, but the staff graciously served us steaming hot Misal Thali. Since it was raining, we also enjoyed hot kanda bhaji (onion fritters) and Peru (guava) ice cream, which was unique and absolutely delicious. I loved my visit here for two reasons: the yummy food and the visit to the Pakshyanchi Wadi (bird park).

Built on a guava farm, this place is located approximately 35 km from Nashik Road railway station and 6.1 km from Gangapur Road. Peruchi Wadi is an ideal one-day picnic spot near Mumbai for family and friends. If youre a Misal lover traveling with kids, this isn’t just a restaurant—it’s an amusement park! The bird park features amazing species of animals and birds. My little one was thrilled to visit the bird park and had a wonderful time.
